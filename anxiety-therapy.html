Our Approach
The goal is not to eliminate all anxiety. Some anxiety is functional. The goal is to reduce the anxiety that limits your life and build a more grounded relationship with your own nervous system.

Anxiety is not just a feeling. For many people it is a pattern that shapes decisions, relationships, sleep, and the ability to be present from one day to the next.

Anxiety therapy at Hope & Love is person-centred and practical. We work to understand the patterns beneath your anxiety: the thoughts, interpretations, and responses that maintain it, and develop strategies to reduce its hold on your life. This is not about learning to white-knuckle your way through hard moments. It is about understanding what is underneath them.

Approaches may include Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), mindfulness-based techniques, Internal Family Systems (IFS), and trauma-informed support where relevant.

What to Expect

Anxiety therapy, clearly explained

Who it is for

  • People whose worry, overthinking, or dread will not switch off
  • People experiencing panic attacks or intense physical anxiety
  • People avoiding situations, people, or opportunities out of fear
  • People caught in intrusive thoughts, compulsions, or reassurance-seeking
  • People whose anxiety is rooted in trauma or past experiences
  • People managing anxiety alongside addiction or depression

What sessions cover

  • Understanding your anxiety pattern: triggers, thoughts, and responses
  • Practical tools for settling the body and the nervous system
  • Changing your relationship with anxious thoughts, not just fighting them
  • Gradually reclaiming what anxiety has taken off the table
  • Working with deeper roots where they exist, at your pace

Sessions are 50 minutes, in-person or via secure video. You set the pace. Nothing is pushed faster than you are ready for.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

What types of anxiety does Hope & Love treat?
We support individuals dealing with generalized anxiety, social anxiety, panic disorder, OCD, health anxiety, and anxiety connected to trauma or past experiences. We also support people whose anxiety is intertwined with addiction, depression, or relationship difficulties.
Is anxiety therapy available virtually in Ontario?
Yes. All anxiety therapy services are available via secure video throughout Ontario. Virtual sessions are just as effective as in-person for most anxiety presentations, and for some people, being in a familiar space makes it easier to engage.
How long does anxiety therapy take?
It depends on the nature and duration of the anxiety, your goals, and how it connects to other areas of your life. Some people notice meaningful change within a few months. Others benefit from longer-term work. We revisit goals regularly, and therapy is always your choice to continue.
What if my anxiety is connected to addiction?
Anxiety and addiction frequently occur together. Many people use substances or compulsive behaviours to manage anxiety. When both are present, addressing them together in therapy is more effective than treating either in isolation. Matt has specific experience working with this combination.
