Couples Therapy in Prince Edward County

In-person and virtual couples therapy in the County — Picton, Wellington, Bloomfield, and beyond — with Matt Harris, Registered Psychotherapist.

Picton · Wellington · Bloomfield In-Person & Virtual Sessions Registered Psychotherapist

Life in Prince Edward County can put particular pressure on a relationship. The seasonal economy means many couples run flat out through the summer and then face a long, quiet winter together. Others are navigating a move to the County, a second home and a divided life, retirement, or the isolation that can settle in once the visitors leave. All of it lands on the relationship.

Hope & Love offers couples therapy right in the County, in person or by secure video, to help partners reconnect and find their footing again.

What couples come in for

  • Communication that has narrowed to logistics, or to the same argument
  • Seasonal strain — long hours in season, then too much silence out of it
  • Rebuilding trust after betrayal, secrecy, or addiction
  • Big transitions — relocation, retirement, or a decision about the future

How the work is done

Matt is trained in Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT), which helps couples step out of blame-and-withdrawal cycles and understand what each partner is really reaching for. Sessions are balanced and non-blaming, so both people leave feeling heard.

In-person in the County or virtual year-round

Prince Edward County is one of Hope & Love’s two in-person locations, so you can meet together in the County rather than driving off-island. Secure video is available year-round — especially practical once the season turns — and reaches couples across the County, Belleville, and the wider Quinte region.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Can we come to couples therapy in person in the County?
Yes. Prince Edward County is one of Hope & Love’s two in-person locations. You can meet together in the County, or join by secure video — year-round, and including when you are in different places.
Is therapy available in the County through the off-season?
Yes. Secure video sessions continue year-round, which many couples find easier once the season winds down and schedules and driving conditions change.
Does insurance cover couples therapy?
Many Ontario extended health benefit plans cover sessions with a Registered Psychotherapist. Matt provides detailed receipts for insurance submission. Check your plan for "psychotherapy" or "registered psychotherapist" coverage.
