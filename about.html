Your Therapist

Matt Harris

Registered Psychotherapist • Addiction & Couples Specialist • Ontario

Matt Harris is a Registered Psychotherapist in Ontario who works with people navigating addiction and relationship crisis. He believes that beneath the patterns, the secrecy, and the shame, there is still a person capable of real change, and that most people need honesty and safety more than they need advice.

His practice is warm, direct, and grounded in clinical rigour. He takes the spiritual, emotional, and relational sides of recovery seriously because they tend to be where the real work happens.

Background and training

Matt has worked in community and private practice settings across Ontario, supporting individuals and couples through some of the most difficult periods of their lives. He has seen what genuine healing looks like, and what makes it possible.

He draws from several evidence-based approaches, using whichever fits the person and the problem:

Motivational Interviewing Cognitive Behavioural Therapy Emotionally Focused Therapy Trauma-Informed Care Harm Reduction Faith-Informed Support Shame-Informed Practice

Why addiction and relationships

Addiction touches far more than behaviour. It gets into intimacy, communication, identity, and the way people feel about themselves. Matt chose to work at the intersection of addiction and relationships because the two are rarely separate, and because change in one area almost always changes the other.

"Hope & Love is not just about stopping addictive behaviour. It is about healing the disconnection that addiction creates, for the person struggling, for the partner, and for the relationship."

A note on faith

Matt is open to spiritual reflection and faith as part of the healing process, for clients who want that. It is never assumed or imposed. For some people it is central to their recovery. For others it plays no role at all. Either way is welcome here.

Working with Matt

Sessions are collaborative and honest. Matt will not tell you what you want to hear, but he will take you seriously, respect your pace, and never promise outcomes he cannot guarantee. The work is hard. The space is safe.

What Guides This Practice

Six values at the heart of Hope & Love

Compassion with truth

We meet people with grace, but we do not avoid reality. Healing requires honesty. Compassion without truth is not compassion, it is avoidance dressed up as kindness.

Person-centred care

People are more than their addiction, their pain, their choices, or their relationship status. Support must honour the whole person, not just the presenting problem.

Accountability without shame

Responsibility matters. So does dignity. People heal best when accountability is paired with safety, clarity, and hope, not condemnation.

Love with boundaries

Love is not denial, rescuing, enabling, or staying in unsafe situations. Healthy love includes boundaries, wisdom, and the courage to ask for support. Boundaries can be an act of love.

Practical hope

Hope is more than a feeling. It becomes therapy, support plans, relapse prevention, communication tools, crisis planning, and next steps. Hope becomes meaningful when it turns into action.

Faith-informed grace

Hope & Love welcomes spiritual reflection, grace, and faith-informed support, while remaining emotionally safe, clinically responsible, and respectful of each client's story. Faith is a resource, never a requirement.

Addiction is not the whole person. The pain is real. The damage matters. But there is still a person worthy of love, accountability, healing, and grace.

— Matt Harris, RP
