Anxiety Therapy in Belleville

In-person and virtual therapy for anxiety, worry, and panic in Belleville and the Quinte region, with Matt Harris, Registered Psychotherapist.

Belleville & Quinte Region In-Person & Virtual Sessions Registered Psychotherapist

Anxiety rarely announces itself. It shows up as a mind that will not switch off at night, a knot in your chest before work, decisions you keep circling, or a low hum of dread you cannot quite explain. In a busy region like Belleville and the Bay of Quinte — work, family, commuting, and everything else — it is easy to keep pushing through until it starts to shape your whole life.

Hope & Love offers focused anxiety therapy for people in Belleville, in person or by secure video. This is anxiety therapy grounded in evidence-based approaches, delivered at a pace that is yours.

How anxiety shows up

  • Generalized worry, overthinking, and difficulty relaxing or sleeping
  • Panic attacks and the fear of them returning
  • Social anxiety and fear of judgment
  • OCD and intrusive thoughts
  • Health anxiety, and anxiety connected to past trauma

How therapy helps

Matt draws on CBT, mindfulness-based techniques, IFS, and, where anxiety is rooted in trauma, EMDR. The goal is not just to manage symptoms but to understand what your anxiety is responding to, so its grip on your daily life loosens over time.

In-person in Belleville or virtual across Quinte

Belleville is one of Hope & Love’s two in-person locations. You can meet face to face here, or choose secure video from anywhere in the region, including Quinte West, Napanee, and Prince Edward County.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Can I see an anxiety therapist in person in Belleville?
Yes. Belleville is one of Hope & Love’s two in-person locations. You can meet face to face, or choose secure video sessions from anywhere in the Quinte region and Ontario.
How long does anxiety therapy take?
It depends on what is driving the anxiety and your goals. Some people notice a shift in a handful of sessions; others work over a longer period on deeper patterns. We revisit your goals regularly, and the pace is always yours.
Does insurance cover anxiety therapy?
Many Ontario extended health benefit plans cover sessions with a Registered Psychotherapist. Matt provides detailed receipts for insurance submission. Check your plan for "psychotherapy" or "registered psychotherapist" coverage.
