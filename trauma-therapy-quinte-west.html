Trauma & PTSD Therapy in Quinte West & Trenton

Trauma-informed therapy for Quinte West, Trenton, and the CFB Trenton community, by secure video with in-person sessions nearby in Belleville. With Matt Harris, Registered Psychotherapist.

Trenton · Frankford · Batawa Virtual · In-Person in Belleville Registered Psychotherapist

Trauma leaves its mark long after the event itself. It can show up as hypervigilance, broken sleep, flashbacks or intrusive memories, emotional numbing, irritability, or a constant sense of being on guard. In a community shaped by 8 Wing / CFB Trenton, these experiences are familiar to many — serving members, veterans, first responders, and the families who live alongside operational stress.

Hope & Love offers trauma-informed therapy for people in Quinte West, by secure video with in-person sessions available nearby in Belleville. The work goes at your pace, with safety and control kept firmly in your hands.

Trauma can look like

  • Hypervigilance, being easily startled, or never fully able to relax
  • Flashbacks, nightmares, or intrusive memories
  • Emotional numbing, disconnection, or avoiding reminders
  • Irritability and anger that strain relationships
  • Trauma connected to anxiety, depression, or substance use

A trauma-informed approach

Matt draws on trauma-informed care and, where appropriate, EMDR — an evidence-based approach for processing traumatic memory. Therapy starts with stabilization and trust, never with pushing you to relive the worst of it before you are ready.

Support alongside military and first-responder care

Hope & Love is a private psychotherapy practice, not a military or Veterans Affairs program. Serving members and veterans can access dedicated care through the base, the Operational Stress Injury (OSI) clinics, and Veterans Affairs Canada — and you are welcome here for the parts of life that sit alongside that, or if you simply prefer private, confidential support.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do you provide therapy for the CFB Trenton military community?
Yes, as a private practice. Hope & Love offers trauma-informed therapy to serving members, veterans, first responders, and their families for the personal side of their lives. For service-related injuries, dedicated care is also available through the base, OSI clinics, and Veterans Affairs Canada.
What is EMDR, and do I have to talk through the trauma in detail?
EMDR is an evidence-based approach that helps the brain process traumatic memory, often without needing to describe every detail aloud. Therapy always begins with stabilization and trust, and moves only at a pace that feels safe to you.
Can I do trauma therapy virtually?
Yes. Secure video sessions are effective for trauma work and are available throughout Quinte West and Ontario. In-person sessions are also available nearby in Belleville if you prefer to meet face to face.
