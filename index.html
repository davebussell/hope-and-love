Our Story
Addiction is not the whole person. Beneath the patterns, shame, and disconnection, there is still someone worthy of love, healing, grace, and hope.

Addiction can make people feel like strangers to themselves. And to the people who love them most.

For the person caught in it, hope starts to feel buried under guilt, secrecy, and shame. The gap between who you are and who you want to be gets harder to close. For the partner on the other side, loving someone through addiction can quietly become one of the hardest things you have ever done.

Hope & Love exists for both.

Through addiction therapy and couples counselling, we help people rebuild trust, understand what keeps them stuck, and find a real path forward, whether together or on their own.

The damage is real. But it does not have to be the end of the story.

Your Therapist

Matt Harris

Registered Psychotherapist • Addiction & Couples Specialist • Ontario

Matt brings warmth and clinical expertise to every session. He believes healing requires honesty and safety, and that genuine change is possible for anyone willing to do the work.

Specializing in addiction therapy and couples counselling, Matt draws on evidence-based approaches while holding space for the spiritual, emotional, and relational dimensions of recovery. He does not promise outcomes. He offers a grounded, compassionate, and practical path forward.

How We Work

Therapy that meets you where you are

Step 01

Free 20-minute consultation

No paperwork, no commitment. We get to know each other and make sure working together is the right fit before anything else.

Step 02

Personalized support plan

Your story is your own. Together we build a clear, collaborative plan grounded in your goals, your safety, and your lived experience.

Step 03

Ongoing sessions, practical progress

Weekly or bi-weekly sessions, in-person or secure video, with consistent support and honest accountability.

Hope is not just a feeling. Hope can look like booking therapy, setting a boundary, having an honest conversation, or taking one small step toward healing today.

— Matt Harris, RP

A note on safety: Hope does not mean ignoring harm, minimizing betrayal, or staying in unsafe situations. Relationship repair is only considered where safety, willingness, and support are present. Not every relationship can or should be restored, and we will never pressure you toward an outcome that is not right for you.
