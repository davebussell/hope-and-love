Person-First Support

Drug Addiction Therapy

Confidential, person-centred therapy for individuals affected by drug use. You are more than a diagnosis, a decision, or a pattern. Healing begins with honesty and support — not judgment.

At Hope & Love, we use person-first language — "a person struggling with drug use" rather than "an addict" — because language shapes how people see themselves, and how they see themselves shapes how they heal. Drug use is a pattern with identifiable psychological, emotional, and social drivers. Therapy helps make those drivers visible and creates a path toward sustainable change.

Therapy for drug use is not a substitute for medically supervised detox or residential treatment. It is the ongoing psychological and relational work that supports recovery — before, alongside, or after more intensive care.

What therapy for drug use addresses

  • Understanding the emotional, trauma-related, or situational drivers of the drug use
  • Shame and identity — separating the person from the pattern
  • Harm reduction and safety planning for people not yet at abstinence
  • Relapse prevention and resilience in early and sustained recovery
  • Relationships, communication, and the relational impact of drug use
  • Co-occurring mental health challenges such as anxiety, depression, or trauma
  • Building a life that supports recovery — values, purpose, and connection

When higher levels of care may be needed

Therapy is outpatient and does not involve medical monitoring. If you are experiencing physical withdrawal symptoms, need medically supervised detox, or require intensive residential support, Matt will help you understand your options and connect you with appropriate resources. Therapy can then run alongside or follow that care.

Drug use and relationships

Drug use often affects relationships significantly — trust, communication, financial stability, and parenting. Hope & Love offers individual therapy for the person struggling and couples or partner support therapy for those affected by someone else's drug use. Both sides deserve support.

Harm reduction and abstinence

Therapy at Hope & Love is not prescriptive about recovery goals. Some people work toward full abstinence. Others work toward harm reduction — using less, using more safely, or managing the impact on their life. Matt will meet you where you are and work toward goals that are yours.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to be in recovery to start therapy?
No. Many people begin therapy while still using. Therapy can help you understand your relationship with drugs, explore your ambivalence about change, and develop a plan at a pace that is realistic for your situation. You do not need to be ready to stop before you reach out.
Can therapy help with prescription drug use or opioids?
Yes. Therapy is appropriate for any pattern of drug use that is causing you distress or harm — including prescribed medications that have become dependent or compulsive. Matt will work with your full picture and, where medically relevant, recommend coordination with a physician.
What is the difference between therapy and rehab?
Rehabilitation programs are typically intensive, residential, or day-treatment programs that provide medical monitoring, structured programming, and peer support. Therapy is outpatient and ongoing — a professional relationship focused on the psychological and relational work of recovery. Both can be valuable, and often complement each other.
Is everything I share about my drug use confidential?
Yes. Therapy is confidential. The exceptions are narrow and legally defined — imminent danger to yourself or others, or certain disclosures involving children at risk. Outside those exceptions, what you share in session stays in session.

Hope & Love is not a crisis or detox service. If you need emergency medical care for withdrawal or overdose, call 911 immediately. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988. For overdose prevention resources in Ontario, visit ontario.ca/drugs.
