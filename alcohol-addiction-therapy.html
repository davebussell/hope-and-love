Alcohol Addiction Therapy

Therapeutic support for alcohol use disorder, focused on the patterns and relationships underneath alcohol use, differentiated from detox and rehab, and built around your goals.

Alcohol use disorder is one of the most common and most misunderstood forms of addiction. Many people struggle with alcohol use for years before seeking help, and when they do, they are often met with shame, labels, or a one-size-fits-all approach that does not fit their life.

Therapy for alcohol use disorder at Hope & Love is individualized, non-judgmental, and focused on understanding what drives the use, not just managing the behaviour. It works alongside medical care where needed, and can support harm reduction goals as well as abstinence.

Therapy vs. detox, AA, and rehab

It is important to be clear about what therapy is and is not:

  • Therapy is ongoing outpatient support focused on the psychological, emotional, and relational aspects of alcohol use. It addresses the why, not just the what.
  • Detox is medically supervised withdrawal support. If you drink heavily and want to stop, medical supervision may be necessary. Matt can help you connect with appropriate detox resources.
  • AA and peer support are community-based programs grounded in mutual support and accountability. Therapy and peer support are often complementary, not competing.
  • Residential rehab is intensive, structured, and full-time. Outpatient therapy can precede, follow, or run alongside residential treatment.

What alcohol therapy explores

  • The emotional and situational triggers driving your drinking
  • Patterns of avoidance, stress, and regulation that alcohol has been managing
  • Co-occurring anxiety, depression, trauma, or relationship difficulties
  • Family and relational impact, including the experience of partners and children
  • Shame, identity, and self-worth in the context of alcohol use
  • Practical tools for harm reduction, cravings, and urge management
  • Long-term recovery planning and relapse prevention

Abstinence or harm reduction, your goals matter

Therapy at Hope & Love is not prescriptive. Some people work toward full abstinence. Others work toward reducing harm, drinking less, drinking more safely, or managing the impact on their relationships and health. Matt will work with your goals, while being honest when a goal carries risk.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to have stopped drinking before I start therapy?
No. Many people begin therapy while still drinking. Therapy can help you understand your relationship with alcohol, explore your readiness for change, and work toward goals at a pace that is realistic for your situation.
Is it safe to stop drinking on my own?
It depends on the severity and duration of use. Heavy, long-term alcohol use can cause dangerous withdrawal symptoms. If you are physically dependent, speak with a physician before stopping. Matt can help you think through this and connect you with appropriate medical resources.
Can I do alcohol therapy online?
Yes. Virtual sessions are available to anyone in Ontario and are appropriate for most alcohol therapy work. All you need is a private space and a stable internet connection.
My partner drinks too much. Can I come to therapy about their drinking?
Yes. Partner and family support therapy is available for people affected by someone else's alcohol use. You do not have to be the person struggling to deserve professional support.

Hope & Love does not provide medically supervised detox. If you experience withdrawal symptoms (tremors, sweating, confusion, seizure risk) when you stop drinking, seek medical care immediately or call 911. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988.
