Our Approach
Depression can make reaching out feel impossible. That is not a sign you are not ready — it is a symptom. Starting with a free consultation requires almost nothing. And it can change everything.

Depression is more than sadness. It is a heaviness that can make the simplest tasks feel impossible, drain meaning from things that once mattered, and create a sense of distance from your own life.

Depression therapy at Hope & Love is collaborative and person-centred. We work to understand what is maintaining your depression — the patterns of thought, behaviour, and disconnection that keep it in place — and develop practical strategies for change alongside deeper insight into what the depression is connected to.

Approaches may include Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), behavioural activation, Internal Family Systems (IFS), and compassion-focused therapy. Sessions move at your pace, and nothing is pushed before you are ready.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Is depression therapy different from medication?
Therapy and medication are different approaches, each with evidence behind them. Some people benefit most from therapy alone, some from medication alone, and some from a combination. We can discuss what makes sense for your situation and support referrals where helpful.
How long does depression therapy take?
It depends on the depth and duration of the depression, your goals, and how it connects to other areas of your life. Some clients experience significant improvement within a few months. Others benefit from longer-term work. We revisit goals regularly and therapy is always your choice to continue.
Is depression therapy available virtually in Ontario?
Yes. All services are available via secure video across Ontario. Virtual therapy is effective for depression and is often easier to access when motivation and energy are low — no commute, no waiting room.
What if my depression is connected to addiction?
Depression and addiction are closely linked. Some people use substances or compulsive behaviours to manage the emptiness of depression. Others find that addiction leads to depression. When both are present, Matt works with them together rather than treating either in isolation.
