Online Addiction Therapy

Secure, confidential video therapy for individuals and couples across Ontario, accessible from home, private, and just as effective as in-person care.

Available anywhere in Ontario Secure video platform No waiting rooms Insurance-eligible

Online addiction therapy removes the practical barriers that prevent many people from seeking support, commute, scheduling, stigma, and the vulnerability of walking into a clinic. You can access professional, evidence-based addiction counselling from anywhere in Ontario, in the privacy of your own space.

Research consistently shows that online therapy is as effective as in-person therapy for most mental health and addiction concerns. The therapeutic relationship, the most significant factor in outcomes, translates well to video.

Who online therapy is for

  • Anyone in Ontario who prefers the privacy and convenience of virtual sessions
  • People whose addiction involves sensitive topics, pornography, sex, infidelity, who prefer not to attend a clinic
  • Individuals or couples outside major urban centres with limited local access
  • People with demanding schedules or caregiving responsibilities
  • Those who have tried in-person therapy and found access barriers difficult
  • Couples in different locations who want to attend sessions together

How online addiction therapy works

Sessions are conducted via a secure, encrypted video platform, similar to a video call, but built for confidentiality and clinical use. All you need is a stable internet connection and a private space.

  • Book a free 20-minute consultation by phone or video
  • Matt will confirm your session format preference and arrange access
  • Sessions are 50 minutes and follow the same structure as in-person care
  • All documentation and receipts are provided digitally for insurance claims

What can be covered online

Online therapy is appropriate for the full range of addiction counselling services offered at Hope & Love:

  • Individual addiction therapy, substance use, gambling, pornography, sex, technology
  • Couples therapy for addiction and relationship repair
  • Partner support therapy
  • Shame and self-worth counselling
  • Relapse prevention and recovery support

Privacy and confidentiality

Online sessions are fully confidential. The video platform is encrypted and PHIPA-compliant. Your session content, name, and identity are protected under the same professional and legal standards as in-person therapy.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Is online therapy as effective as in-person therapy?
Yes. Research consistently shows equivalent outcomes for most therapeutic modalities, including addiction counselling. The therapeutic relationship, the primary factor in outcomes, translates well to video. Many clients find virtual sessions easier to be open in, given the comfort of their own space.
What do I need to access online sessions?
A stable internet connection, a private space, and a device with a camera and microphone (phone, tablet, or computer). Matt will send a secure link before each session.
Can couples attend online sessions together?
Yes. Both partners can join from the same location or from separate devices. Matt will arrange the session format based on your situation.
Is online therapy covered by insurance?
Many extended health benefit plans in Ontario cover registered psychotherapy regardless of format, including virtual sessions. Check your plan for "psychotherapy" or "registered psychotherapist" coverage. Matt provides detailed receipts for insurance submission.
