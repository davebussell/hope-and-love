Our Approach
Addiction is not the whole person. Beneath the patterns, shame, and disconnection, there is still someone worthy of love, healing, and genuine hope.

Addiction can make people feel like strangers to themselves, and to the people who love them most.

For the person caught in it, hope starts to feel buried under guilt, secrecy, and shame. The gap between who you are and who you want to be gets harder to close. For the partner on the other side, loving someone through addiction can quietly become one of the hardest things you have ever done.

Hope & Love exists for both.

Addiction therapy here is not about condemnation. It is a confidential, ongoing therapeutic relationship that helps people understand the roots of their dependency, develop healthier patterns, and move toward a life they value. You do not need to have hit rock bottom to begin. The damage is real, but it does not have to be the end of the story.

What to Expect

Addiction therapy, clearly explained

Who it is for

  • People struggling with substance use - alcohol, cannabis, prescription drugs, stimulants
  • People facing behavioural addictions - pornography, gambling, sex, technology
  • Partners or family members of someone struggling with addiction
  • Couples dealing with the fallout of addiction on the relationship
  • People in early recovery who want ongoing therapeutic support
  • People who have relapsed and are looking for a non-shaming path forward

What sessions cover

  • Understanding what is driving the behaviour, not just the behaviour itself
  • Shame, identity, and self-worth in the context of addiction
  • Communication, relationships, and the impact addiction has had on others
  • Relapse prevention, harm reduction, and safety planning
  • Grief, loss, and the process of reconnecting with who you are

Sessions are 50 minutes, in-person or via secure video. You set the pace. Nothing is pushed faster than you are ready for.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do I have to want to stop to come to therapy?
No. Many people arrive in addiction therapy feeling ambivalent, part of them wanting to change and part of them not ready. That ambivalence is something we work with directly. You do not need to have your mind made up before you reach out.
Is addiction therapy the same as going to rehab?
No. Therapy is outpatient, confidential, and ongoing. Rehab and residential treatment are intensive programs that often precede or run alongside therapy. If you need a higher level of care, Matt will help you understand your options and connect you with appropriate resources.
What if I relapse during therapy?
Relapse can be part of recovery. It does not mean therapy has failed, and it is not a reason to stop. If a relapse happens, you bring it to session. We explore what it tells us and adjust the plan together.
Is everything I share in therapy confidential?
Yes, with a small number of legally required exceptions (imminent danger to you or others). Matt will explain these clearly at the start of therapy. Outside those exceptions, nothing leaves the session.

Hope & Love is not a crisis service. If you are in immediate danger, call 911. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988.
