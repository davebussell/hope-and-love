Addiction Therapy

Person-centred therapy for individuals and couples navigating addiction, without judgment, without shame, and without pressure. Healing begins where you are.

Addiction therapy is not the same as detox, residential treatment, or rehab. It is a confidential, ongoing therapeutic relationship that helps people understand the roots of their dependency, develop healthier patterns, and move toward a life they value.

At Hope & Love, we do not meet people facing addiction with condemnation. We meet them with honesty, compassion, and a practical path forward. Addiction may have shaped your choices, but it does not define your worth.

Who addiction therapy is for

You do not need to have hit "rock bottom" to benefit from therapy. Many people come to counselling feeling ambivalent, confused, or unsure whether their patterns are a problem. Wherever you are is a valid place to begin.

  • People struggling with substance use, alcohol, cannabis, prescription drugs, stimulants
  • People facing behavioural addictions, pornography, gambling, sex, technology
  • Partners or family members of someone struggling with addiction
  • Couples dealing with the fallout of addiction on the relationship
  • People in early recovery who want ongoing therapeutic support
  • People who have relapsed and are looking for a non-shaming path forward

What addiction therapy covers

Sessions are collaborative. We work at a pace that is yours. Depending on your situation, therapy may explore:

  • Understanding what is driving the behaviour, not just the behaviour itself
  • Shame, identity, and self-worth in the context of addiction
  • Communication, relationships, and the impact addiction has had on the people around you
  • Relapse prevention, harm reduction, and safety planning
  • Grief, loss, and the process of reconnecting with who you are
  • Faith and spiritual dimensions of recovery, for those who want to explore them

Addiction therapy vs. other forms of care

Therapy is not a substitute for medically supervised detox, residential treatment, or crisis services. If you need medical support for withdrawal or are in a crisis, Matt will help point you toward the right resources. Therapy tends to work best alongside or after medical stabilization, and for most people it is a core part of lasting recovery.

What to expect in sessions

Sessions are 50 minutes. You set the pace. Matt may ask questions, reflect back what he is hearing, or introduce practical tools, but the direction is always collaborative. You are never pushed toward a disclosure or pace you are not ready for.

Sessions are available in-person in Ontario and via secure, confidential video for clients anywhere in the province.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Do I have to want to stop to come to therapy?
No. Many people arrive in addiction therapy feeling ambivalent, part of them wants to change, part of them is not ready. That ambivalence is something we work with directly. You do not need to have your mind made up before you reach out.
Is addiction therapy the same as going to rehab?
No. Therapy is outpatient, confidential, and ongoing. Rehab and residential treatment are intensive programs that often precede or run alongside therapy. If you need a higher level of care, Matt will help you understand your options and connect you with appropriate resources.
What if I relapse during therapy?
Relapse can be part of recovery. It does not mean therapy has failed, and it is not a reason to stop. If a relapse happens, you bring it to session. We explore what it tells us and adjust the plan together.
Is everything I share in therapy confidential?
Yes, with a small number of legally required exceptions (imminent danger to you or others). Matt will explain these clearly at the start of therapy. Outside those exceptions, nothing leaves the session.

Hope & Love is not a crisis service. If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 911. For mental health crisis support, call or text 988.
